DIVVYUP & GROW — Fractional Real Estate Investment Platform

DivvyUp & Grow was created to make luxury real estate investment accessible to a broader group of investors. Traditionally, high-value properties are limited to ultra-wealthy buyers due to large capital requirements and complex ownership structures.

The platform introduces a fractional ownership model where each luxury property is divided into 8 investment shares, allowing individuals to participate in premium real estate while lowering the entry barrier. The goal was to combine institutional-grade investing with a seamless digital experience — giving investors transparency, liquidity, and simplified ownership.

I helped co-design the digital asset and portfolio management app — an exclusive access platform where clients can view available properties, invest fractionally, and track their investments. I also led brand placement and business positioning to establish DivvyUp's identity in the PropTech space.

MEME BATTLE ARENA — 2D Battle Royale

Meme Battle Arena was a Web3-powered 2D battle royale game developed at KA Labs, designed to merge competitive gaming with decentralised finance mechanics. The platform introduced a win-to-earn model where players could stake tokens to enter matches and compete for rewards distributed directly from prize pools.

The blockchain mechanics and economic structure were researched and developed at Get Thrifty LLP, using the 1-UP token as the core asset powering gameplay participation and rewards. By combining competitive gameplay with tokenised incentives, the platform aimed to create a transparent and self-sustaining in-game economy.

Following its development phase, the project transitioned to new ownership and was later rebranded as Turbo Battle Arena.
